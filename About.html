About our project

This application was created for the end of the year project by the Information Resource Management class of 2019-2020 at Carleton University.

BibSearch is an application that helps users navigate libraries (e.g. the MacOdrum Library). The application will let users search a database for a specific book. The application then takes the user’s GPS location and provides directions based on the book’s presumed location on the shelf -- like a Google Maps for libraries. This application will employ indoor navigation to achieve this goal. BibSearch will be developed for Android, using Java.

This application is meant primarily for the use of library patrons. Patrons, especially new students, are often very confused by the library building and the Library of Congress call number system. This application will allow them to quickly and easily locate the material they need. As a result, library staff will be able to utilize their time better by answering more in-depth questions, which is especially helpful during busy periods.

Who are we?
We're a team composed of IRM students at Carleton University: Alejandro Garcia, Alex Loupova, Ishani Saha-Roy, Sarah Barssoum and Vincent Bugler. The IRM program's goal is to merge the organizational and analytical skills of the library technician profession with a firm grasp of information technology skills. We believe that our application reflects a proper merging of these two concepts, as it put much of what we'd learnt over the course of our degree to the test.

Dr.Omair Shafiq is a professor and IRM coordinator for the School of Information Technology at Carleton University. Dr.Shafiq was in charge of supervising the project and the students while they worked on the application.
